About Rosacea

Rosacea is a chronic skin condition causing facial redness, visible blood vessels, and sometimes small red bumps. Clinical trials investigate topical treatments, oral medications, laser therapies, and novel approaches to control inflammation.

Common Symptoms

Facial redness and flushing, visible blood vessels (telangiectasias), red bumps and pus-filled lesions, eye irritation (ocular rosacea), skin thickening (especially nose), burning or stinging

Risk Factors

Fair skin, family history, age (30-50), female gender, sun exposure, certain triggers (spicy foods, alcohol, temperature extremes), Demodex mites

Treatment Overview

Treatment includes topical medications (metronidazole, ivermectin, azelaic acid), oral antibiotics (doxycycline), laser therapy, and trigger avoidance. Clinical trials explore newer topical formulations, targeted anti-inflammatory agents, and improved laser technologies.

Why Participate?

Rosacea clinical trials help develop more effective treatments that can reduce facial redness and prevent disease progression with fewer side effects.
