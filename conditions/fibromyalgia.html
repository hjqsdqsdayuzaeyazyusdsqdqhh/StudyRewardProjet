About Fibromyalgia

Fibromyalgia is a chronic condition characterized by widespread musculoskeletal pain, fatigue, and tender points. Clinical trials investigate new pain medications, nerve modulation therapies, and integrative treatment approaches.

Common Symptoms

Widespread pain, fatigue, sleep disturbances, cognitive difficulty (fibro fog), headaches, irritable bowel syndrome, anxiety, depression, morning stiffness

Risk Factors

Family history, female gender, trauma or injury, infections, autoimmune conditions, repetitive stress injuries, emotional distress

Treatment Overview

Treatment includes pain medications (pregabalin, duloxetine), exercise therapy, cognitive behavioral therapy, and sleep management. Clinical trials explore novel neuropathic pain agents, low-dose naltrexone, and non-invasive neuromodulation techniques.

Why Participate?

Fibromyalgia clinical trials are crucial for developing more effective treatments for a condition where many patients have inadequate symptom relief.
