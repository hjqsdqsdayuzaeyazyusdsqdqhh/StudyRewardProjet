About Melanoma

Melanoma is the most serious type of skin cancer, developing from pigment-producing melanocytes. Clinical trials investigate checkpoint immunotherapies, targeted therapies for BRAF-mutated melanoma, and innovative combination treatment strategies.

Common Symptoms

New pigmented spot or existing mole that changes size, shape, or color, asymmetry or irregular borders, multiple colors, diameter larger than pencil eraser, evolution over time, ulceration or bleeding

Risk Factors

UV exposure (sun and tanning beds), fair skin, many moles, family history, personal history of skin cancer, weakened immune system, age, male gender

Treatment Overview

Treatment includes surgical excision, checkpoint inhibitors (pembrolizumab, nivolumab, ipilimumab), targeted therapy for BRAF mutations (dabrafenib, trametinib), and radiation for advanced disease. Clinical trials explore neoadjuvant immunotherapy combinations, tumor-infiltrating lymphocyte (TIL) therapy, and novel vaccine approaches.

Why Participate?

Melanoma clinical trials have revolutionized treatment with immunotherapies that achieve durable responses, and continue pushing toward more effective combination strategies.
