About Sinusitis

Sinusitis is inflammation of sinus cavities causing facial pain, congestion, and difficulty breathing. Clinical trials investigate new antibiotics, corticosteroid formulations, biologic therapies for chronic sinusitis, and minimally invasive surgical techniques.

Common Symptoms

Facial pressure and pain, nasal congestion, thick nasal discharge, reduced sense of smell, headache, cough, fever, bad breath, tooth pain, fatigue

Risk Factors

Seasonal allergies, nasal polyps, asthma, smoking, dental infections, deviated septum, immune deficiency, frequent respiratory infections

Treatment Overview

Treatment includes saline irrigation, nasal corticosteroid sprays, decongestants, antibiotics for bacterial infection, and surgery for chronic cases. Clinical trials explore biologic therapies (dupilumab, omalizumab) for chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyps and novel drug-eluting sinus stents.

Why Participate?

Sinusitis clinical trials help develop more targeted treatments that reduce the need for surgery and improve long-term symptom control.
