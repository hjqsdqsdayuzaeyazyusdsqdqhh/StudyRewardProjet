About Osteoarthritis

Osteoarthritis is a degenerative joint disease where cartilage breaks down over time, causing pain and stiffness. Clinical trials investigate disease-modifying osteoarthritis drugs, regenerative therapies, and pain management innovations.

Common Symptoms

Joint pain during movement, morning stiffness, tenderness, loss of flexibility, grating sensation, bone spurs, joint swelling

Risk Factors

Age, joint injury or overuse, obesity, family history, gender (more common in women), bone deformities, certain metabolic conditions

Treatment Overview

Treatment includes NSAIDs, acetaminophen, corticosteroid injections, hyaluronic acid injections, physical therapy, and joint replacement surgery. Clinical trials explore nerve growth factor inhibitors, stem cell therapies, and novel anti-inflammatory agents.

Why Participate?

Osteoarthritis clinical trials help develop treatments that can modify disease progression rather than just manage symptoms, addressing a major unmet medical need.
