About Infertility

Infertility is the inability to conceive after one year of regular unprotected intercourse, affecting both men and women. Clinical trials investigate new assisted reproductive technologies, fertility preservation methods, and treatments for underlying causes.

Common Symptoms

Difficulty conceiving, irregular menstrual cycles, hormonal imbalances, ovulation disorders, low sperm count or motility, erectile dysfunction, age-related fertility decline

Risk Factors

Age (especially over 35), endometriosis, PCOS, tubal blockage, low sperm count, smoking, obesity, stress, sexually transmitted infections

Treatment Overview

Treatment includes fertility medications (clomiphene, gonadotropins), intrauterine insemination (IUI), in vitro fertilization (IVF), intracytoplasmic sperm injection (ICSI), and donor egg or sperm. Clinical trials explore AI-enhanced embryo selection, time-lapse imaging, and endometrial receptivity testing.

Why Participate?

Infertility clinical trials help improve success rates of fertility treatments and develop new options for those who have not succeeded with current approaches.
