About Hernia

A hernia occurs when an organ pushes through an opening in the muscle or tissue that holds it in place, most commonly in the abdomen. Clinical trials investigate new mesh materials, laparoscopic and robotic repair techniques, and enhanced recovery protocols.

Common Symptoms

Bulge or lump in the affected area, aching or burning sensation, discomfort with lifting or bending, heaviness or pressure, pain in the groin or scrotum (inguinal hernia)

Risk Factors

Age, male gender, family history, obesity, heavy lifting, chronic cough, constipation, pregnancy, prior surgery, connective tissue disorders

Treatment Overview

Treatment typically involves surgical repair with or without mesh, using open, laparoscopic, or robotic approaches. Clinical trials explore biologic and synthetic mesh materials, tension-free repair techniques, and single-incision laparoscopic approaches for faster recovery.

Why Participate?

Hernia clinical trials advance surgical techniques and mesh technologies to reduce recurrence rates and improve postoperative recovery.
