About Narcolepsy

Narcolepsy is a chronic sleep disorder causing overwhelming daytime drowsiness and sudden sleep attacks. Clinical trials investigate novel wake-promoting agents, orexin receptor agonists, and treatments for cataplexy and other symptoms.

Common Symptoms

Excessive daytime sleepiness, sudden loss of muscle tone (cataplexy), sleep paralysis, hallucinations upon falling asleep, disrupted nighttime sleep, automatic behaviors

Risk Factors

Family history, autoimmune conditions, certain infections (H1N1), head injury, age (usually 10-30), genetic markers (HLA-DQB1*0602)

Treatment Overview

Treatment includes stimulants (modafinil, armodafinil), sodium oxybate, antidepressants for cataplexy, and scheduled naps. Clinical trials explore novel orexin receptor agonists, histamine H3 receptor inverse agonists, and immunotherapy approaches.

Why Participate?

Narcolepsy clinical trials help develop more effective treatments with better tolerability for managing sleep attacks and cataplexy symptoms.
