About Stroke

A stroke occurs when blood supply to part of the brain is interrupted, causing brain cell death and neurological deficits. Clinical trials investigate acute interventions, neuroprotective agents, and rehabilitation strategies to improve recovery.

Common Symptoms

Sudden numbness or weakness on one side, confusion, trouble speaking, vision problems, dizziness, severe headache with no known cause, difficulty walking

Risk Factors

High blood pressure, smoking, diabetes, high cholesterol, obesity, atrial fibrillation, age, family history, sedentary lifestyle

Treatment Overview

Acute treatment includes clot-dissolving medications (tPA), mechanical thrombectomy, and supportive care. Rehabilitation involves physical, occupational, and speech therapy. Clinical trials explore neuroprotective drugs, stem cell therapies, and brain-computer interfaces for recovery.

Why Participate?

Stroke clinical trials are critical for developing treatments that can minimize brain damage and improve functional recovery after stroke.
