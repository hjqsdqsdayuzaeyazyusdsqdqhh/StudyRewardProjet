About Macular Degeneration

Age-related macular degeneration (AMD) is an eye condition causing central vision loss due to damage to the macula. Clinical trials investigate anti-VEGF therapies, gene therapies, stem cell treatments, and implantable drug delivery systems.

Common Symptoms

Blurred central vision, difficulty reading or recognizing faces, dark spots in central vision, distorted vision (straight lines appear wavy), decreased color intensity

Risk Factors

Age (over 50), family history, smoking, obesity, cardiovascular disease, high blood pressure, light eye color, prolonged sun exposure

Treatment Overview

Treatment includes anti-VEGF injections, photodynamic therapy, laser photocoagulation, and nutritional supplements for dry AMD. Clinical trials explore gene therapies for wet AMD, complement inhibitors for geographic atrophy, and port delivery systems for sustained drug release.

Why Participate?

Macular degeneration clinical trials are vital for developing treatments that can preserve vision and prevent blindness in the aging population.
