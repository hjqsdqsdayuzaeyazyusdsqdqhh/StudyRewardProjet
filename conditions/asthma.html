About Asthma

Asthma is a chronic respiratory condition where airways become inflamed and narrow, causing breathing difficulty. Clinical trials explore new inhaler technologies, biologic therapies, and personalized management approaches.

Common Symptoms

Wheezing, coughing, shortness of breath, chest tightness, symptoms worsen at night or with exercise

Risk Factors

Family history, allergies, respiratory infections, occupational exposures, smoking, air pollution, obesity

Treatment Overview

Treatment includes inhaled corticosteroids, bronchodilators, leukotriene modifiers, and biologic therapies. Clinical trials investigate novel biologic targets, improved delivery devices, and precision medicine approaches.

Why Participate?

Asthma clinical trials help develop more effective treatments with fewer side effects and contribute to understanding the underlying mechanisms of airway inflammation.
