About Heart Disease

Heart disease encompasses various conditions affecting the heart and blood vessels, including coronary artery disease, heart failure, and arrhythmias. Clinical trials investigate new medications, devices, surgical techniques, and prevention strategies.

Common Symptoms

Chest pain, shortness of breath, fatigue, irregular heartbeat, dizziness, swelling in legs, fainting

Risk Factors

High blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, diabetes, obesity, family history, age, sedentary lifestyle

Treatment Overview

Treatment includes medications (statins, beta-blockers, ACE inhibitors), lifestyle changes, angioplasty, stent placement, bypass surgery, and cardiac rehabilitation. Clinical trials explore novel therapies and minimally invasive procedures.

Why Participate?

Heart disease clinical trials advance cardiovascular medicine and provide access to innovative treatments that may improve quality of life and outcomes for millions affected by heart conditions.
