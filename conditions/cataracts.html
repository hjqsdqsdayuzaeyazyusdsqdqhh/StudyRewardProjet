About Cataracts

Cataracts are clouding of the eye's lens leading to vision impairment, commonly associated with aging. Clinical trials investigate advanced intraocular lens technologies, surgical techniques, and pharmacological approaches to prevent or delay cataract formation.

Common Symptoms

Blurred or cloudy vision, difficulty with night vision, sensitivity to light and glare, halos around lights, fading colors, double vision in one eye, frequent prescription changes

Risk Factors

Age, family history, diabetes, smoking, prolonged sun exposure, obesity, high blood pressure, previous eye injury or surgery, prolonged corticosteroid use

Treatment Overview

Treatment is surgical removal of the cloudy lens and replacement with an artificial intraocular lens (IOL). Clinical trials explore multifocal and accommodating IOLs, femtosecond laser-assisted surgery, and eye drops that may reverse or prevent cataract formation.

Why Participate?

Cataract clinical trials advance surgical outcomes and vision quality through improved lens technology and less invasive techniques.
