About Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder is a mood disorder characterized by alternating episodes of mania and depression affecting energy and functioning. Clinical trials investigate mood stabilizers, novel antipsychotics, and personalized treatment approaches based on genetic markers.

Common Symptoms

Mood swings between mania (elevated energy, reduced need for sleep, grandiosity) and depression (sadness, low energy, suicidal thoughts), racing thoughts, poor judgment

Risk Factors

Family history, genetic factors, high stress, trauma, substance use, sleep disruption, major life changes

Treatment Overview

Treatment includes mood stabilizers (lithium, valproate), atypical antipsychotics, antidepressants under careful monitoring, and psychotherapy. Clinical trials explore targeted therapies for specific bipolar subtypes and novel mechanisms for mood stabilization.

Why Participate?

Bipolar disorder clinical trials help develop more effective treatments with fewer side effects, addressing the high need for improved mood stabilization options.
