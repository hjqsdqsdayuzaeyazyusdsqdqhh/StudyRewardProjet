Clinical Trial Travel: Tips for Out-of-State Participation

Practical advice for participants who need to travel out of state for clinical trial participation, including logistics and reimbursement.

By Lisa Thompson February 22, 2026

Practical advice for participants who need to travel out of state for clinical trial participation, including logistics and reimbursement.

Planning Your Travel Logistics

Traveling for a clinical trial requires careful planning. Start by confirming what the study sponsor covers, including flights, lodging, meals, and ground transportation. Some trials arrange travel directly, while others reimburse you after the visit. Book refundable options when possible in case appointments are rescheduled, and keep all receipts for reimbursement purposes.

Managing Health Needs on the Road

When traveling for a study, bring all your regular medications, a copy of your medical records, and the research team's contact information. Pack a bag with essentials for any side effects you might experience. If you have dietary restrictions, confirm meal arrangements with the study coordinator ahead of time. Arrange for someone to accompany you if the study involves procedures that may affect your ability to travel home independently.

Understanding Reimbursement Policies

Each trial has its own travel reimbursement policy. Some offer a flat per-diem rate, while others reimburse actual expenses up to a cap. Ask whether mileage to and from the airport, parking fees, and baggage charges are included. Clarify how and when reimbursement is paid, as some sponsors issue payments weeks after the visit. Get all reimbursement terms in writing before you travel.

Key Takeaways

  • Confirm what travel expenses the study sponsor covers before making arrangements.
  • Keep all receipts for reimbursement and choose refundable bookings when possible.
  • Bring medications, medical records, and research team contacts when traveling.
  • Get reimbursement terms in writing and understand the payment timeline before traveling.

Frequently Asked Questions

Many study coordinators can assist with travel arrangements or recommend travel agencies familiar with clinical trial logistics. Ask about this during the screening process.
Contact the research team as soon as possible. Most coordinators build flexibility into schedules and can reschedule visits, but frequent missed appointments may affect your continued participation.
