Clinical Trial Data Privacy: How Your Information Is Protected

Learn how clinical trial data is collected, stored, and protected, including HIPAA compliance and participant confidentiality.

By Attorney Rachel Green March 28, 2026

Learn how clinical trial data is collected, stored, and protected, including HIPAA compliance and participant confidentiality.

Data Protection Standards

Clinical trial data is protected by multiple layers of privacy and security standards. The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) establishes national standards for protecting health information. Research institutions have additional policies and procedures to safeguard participant data.

How Your Data Is Used

Clinical trial data is used to evaluate the safety and effectiveness of the treatment being studied. Data is typically de-identified before being shared with researchers, regulators, and the scientific community. Participants are informed during the consent process about how their data will be collected, used, and shared.

Key Takeaways

  • Participant data is protected by HIPAA and institutional privacy policies.
  • Data is typically de-identified before being shared externally.
  • The informed consent process explains how your data will be used.
  • Participants have rights to access their own health information.

Frequently Asked Questions

Research data is typically kept for years or even decades to allow for long-term analysis and regulatory review. Retention periods are specified in the consent document.
With appropriate de-identification and IRB approval, clinical trial data may be shared with other researchers to advance scientific knowledge.
