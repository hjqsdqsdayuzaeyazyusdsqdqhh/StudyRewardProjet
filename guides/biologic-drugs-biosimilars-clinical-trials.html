Biologic Drugs and Biosimilars in Clinical Trials

What participants should know about biologic drug clinical trials and how biosimilars compare to original biologic therapies.

By Dr. Michael Chen February 28, 2026

What participants should know about biologic drug clinical trials and how biosimilars compare to original biologic therapies.

What Are Biologic Drugs?

Biologic drugs are complex medications made from living organisms rather than chemical synthesis. They include monoclonal antibodies, growth factors, cytokines, and gene therapies. Biologics are used to treat conditions such as rheumatoid arthritis, psoriasis, inflammatory bowel disease, cancer, and diabetes. Because of their complexity, biologic drugs are more difficult to manufacture and test than traditional small-molecule drugs.

Biosimilar Clinical Trials

Biosimilars are biologic drugs that are highly similar to an already approved reference biologic with no clinically meaningful differences. Clinical trials for biosimilars focus on demonstrating similarity rather than de novo efficacy. These studies typically include pharmacokinetic comparisons and confirmatory efficacy trials in one or more sensitive indications. Safety and immunogenicity data are collected to ensure comparable profiles.

What Participants Should Consider

Participants in biologic trials may receive either the reference biologic or the biosimilar, often without knowing which. The most significant consideration is immunogenicity, as biologic drugs can trigger immune responses that produce anti-drug antibodies. Participants are monitored for infusion reactions, injection site reactions, and changes in effectiveness over time.

Key Takeaways

  • Biologic drugs are complex medications derived from living organisms used for chronic and serious conditions.
  • Biosimilar trials focus on demonstrating similarity to an approved reference biologic.
  • Immunogenicity monitoring is critical in biologic and biosimilar clinical trials.
  • Biosimilars offer potential cost savings while providing comparable effectiveness and safety.
