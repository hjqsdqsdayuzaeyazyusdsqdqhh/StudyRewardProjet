Veterans and Clinical Trials: Research for Military Health

Clinical trial opportunities for veterans, military health research priorities, and how service members can contribute to medical advances.

By Dr. James Wilson January 5, 2026

Clinical trial opportunities for veterans, military health research priorities, and how service members can contribute to medical advances.

VA Research and Clinical Trials

The Department of Veterans Affairs operates one of the largest integrated healthcare research programs in the United States. VA research focuses on conditions particularly relevant to veterans including post-traumatic stress disorder, traumatic brain injury, chronic pain, hearing loss, and prosthetic development. VA medical centers across the country conduct thousands of clinical trials each year.

Conditions of Focus in Military Research

Military health research prioritizes conditions prevalent among service members and veterans. These include mental health conditions such as PTSD, depression, and anxiety; physical injuries including limb loss and spinal cord injury; neurological conditions like traumatic brain injury; and exposure-related conditions such as respiratory illness from burn pits and Agent Orange-related cancers.

How Veterans Can Get Involved

Veterans can find research opportunities through VA medical centers, the VA Office of Research and Development, and ClinicalTrials.gov. Participation is open to veterans receiving care through VA as well as those who use private healthcare. Some studies offer compensation, travel reimbursement, and access to innovative treatments not yet widely available.

Key Takeaways

  • The VA operates a large research program focused on conditions relevant to military service.
  • Key research areas include PTSD, TBI, chronic pain, hearing loss, and prosthetics.
  • Veterans can participate in trials through VA medical centers regardless of where they receive care.
  • Military health research directly addresses conditions affecting service members and veterans.
