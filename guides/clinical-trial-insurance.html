Clinical Trial Insurance: What Is Covered?

Learn about insurance coverage for clinical trial participation and what costs are typically covered by the study sponsor.

By Michael Park April 5, 2026

Learn about insurance coverage for clinical trial participation and what costs are typically covered by the study sponsor.

Who Pays for Clinical Trial Costs?

Clinical trial costs are typically divided between the study sponsor and the participant's health insurance. The sponsor usually covers the cost of the experimental treatment, study-related tests and procedures, and any additional monitoring required. Routine medical care that would be provided regardless of trial participation is often billed to insurance.

Insurance Coverage for Trial Participation

Many health insurance plans cover routine care costs associated with clinical trials. The Affordable Care Act requires many insurers to cover routine patient care costs for participants in approved clinical trials. However, insurance coverage varies by plan and state. It is important to verify coverage before enrolling.

Key Takeaways

  • Study sponsors typically cover experimental treatment and study-related procedures.
  • Routine care costs may be billed to health insurance.
  • The Affordable Care Act requires many insurers to cover routine costs for trial participants.
  • Verify insurance coverage for clinical trial participation before enrolling.

Frequently Asked Questions

Many sponsors have programs to assist with uncovered costs. The research team can help you understand your coverage and options.
Some trials accept uninsured participants, but it depends on the study. The research team can discuss specific requirements and available support.
