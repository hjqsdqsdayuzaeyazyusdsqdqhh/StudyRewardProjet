Cancer Prevention Clinical Trials: Reducing Your Risk

Discover cancer prevention clinical trials including vaccine research, lifestyle studies, and chemoprevention approaches.

By Dr. Emily Chen February 5, 2026

What Are Cancer Prevention Trials?

Cancer prevention clinical trials study interventions designed to reduce the risk of developing cancer. These include chemoprevention studies testing medications that may prevent cancer, vaccine trials for cancer-causing viruses like HPV and hepatitis B, lifestyle intervention trials examining diet and exercise effects, and screening trials evaluating new early detection methods.

Chemoprevention and Vaccine Studies

Chemoprevention trials test drugs that may block or reverse the development of cancer cells. Common targets include breast cancer prevention with selective estrogen receptor modulators and colorectal cancer prevention with anti-inflammatory medications. Vaccine prevention trials focus on viruses known to cause cancer, with the HPV vaccine being one of the most successful examples of cancer prevention through vaccination.

Lifestyle and Screening Research

Lifestyle prevention trials examine how diet, exercise, weight management, and smoking cessation affect cancer risk. These studies often require long-term follow-up to measure outcomes. Screening trials evaluate new technologies such as liquid biopsies and advanced imaging to detect cancer at earlier, more treatable stages.

Key Takeaways

  • Cancer prevention trials include chemoprevention, vaccines, lifestyle interventions, and screening studies.
  • The HPV vaccine is a major success story in cancer prevention research.
  • Lifestyle trials examine diet, exercise, and weight management effects on cancer risk.
  • Screening trials aim to detect cancer earlier when treatment is more effective.
