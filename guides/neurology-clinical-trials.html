Neurology Clinical Trials: Alzheimer's and Parkinson's Research

Guide to neurology clinical trials for Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease, multiple sclerosis, and epilepsy.

By Dr. Emily Chen April 10, 2026

Guide to neurology clinical trials for Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease, multiple sclerosis, and epilepsy.

Neurology Research Areas

Neurology clinical trials investigate disorders of the brain and nervous system. Major research areas include Alzheimer's disease and other dementias, Parkinson's disease, multiple sclerosis, epilepsy, migraine, and stroke recovery. Advances in neuroimaging and biomarker research are opening new possibilities for earlier diagnosis and targeted treatments.

Alzheimer's Disease Trials

Alzheimer's research includes prevention trials for at-risk individuals, disease-modifying treatments targeting amyloid and tau proteins, cognitive enhancement approaches, and caregiving interventions. Many Alzheimer's trials require participants to undergo cognitive assessments and brain imaging.

Key Takeaways

  • Neurology trials cover Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, MS, epilepsy, migraine, and stroke.
  • Advances in biomarkers and neuroimaging are enabling earlier and more targeted interventions.
  • Cognitive assessments and brain imaging are common in neurology research.
  • Participation contributes to understanding and treating complex brain disorders.
