Remote and Decentralized Clinical Trials: The New Normal

How remote and decentralized clinical trials work, their benefits for participants, and the technology that makes them possible.

By James Rodriguez March 14, 2026

How remote and decentralized clinical trials work, their benefits for participants, and the technology that makes them possible.

What Are Decentralized Clinical Trials?

Decentralized clinical trials use digital technology to reduce or eliminate the need for participants to visit a physical research site. Instead of traveling to a central location for every visit, participants can complete many study activities from home. These include virtual consultations via video calls, home health visits by mobile nurses, and self-administered tests using connected devices.

Technology Enabling Remote Research

Wearable devices such as smartwatches and continuous glucose monitors can collect real-time health data. Mobile apps support symptom tracking, medication reminders, and direct communication with the research team. Telemedicine platforms enable virtual study visits, and electronic consent tools allow participants to review and sign documents remotely.

Benefits and Challenges of Remote Trials

Remote trials reduce the burden of travel and make participation accessible to people in rural areas or with mobility limitations. They can also accelerate recruitment and improve participant retention. Challenges include ensuring participants have reliable internet access and digital literacy, maintaining data quality from home-based measurements, and preserving the personal connection between participants and research teams.

Key Takeaways

  • Decentralized trials use digital technology to reduce or eliminate on-site visits.
  • Wearables, mobile apps, and telemedicine are key technologies enabling remote research.
  • Remote trials improve access for rural participants and those with limited mobility.
  • Digital literacy and internet access remain challenges for decentralized trial adoption.

Frequently Asked Questions
