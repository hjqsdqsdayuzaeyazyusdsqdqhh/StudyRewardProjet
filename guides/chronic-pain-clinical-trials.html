Chronic Pain Clinical Trials: Pain Management Research

Overview of chronic pain clinical trials including innovative non-opioid treatments and pain management approaches.

By Lisa Thompson March 30, 2026

Overview of chronic pain clinical trials including innovative non-opioid treatments and pain management approaches.

Pain Research Landscape

Chronic pain clinical trials investigate treatments for conditions such as back pain, neuropathic pain, fibromyalgia, arthritis pain, and migraine. Given the opioid crisis, there is a major focus on developing non-opioid pain management alternatives including novel medications, nerve stimulation devices, physical therapy approaches, and psychological interventions.

Types of Pain Studies

Pain research includes pharmacotherapy trials testing new non-opioid analgesics, device trials studying spinal cord stimulation and TENS units, interventional procedure trials for nerve blocks and injections, behavioral trials for cognitive behavioral therapy and mindfulness, and complementary medicine trials for acupuncture and massage.

Key Takeaways

  • Chronic pain research is increasingly focused on non-opioid alternatives.
  • Pain studies cover pharmacotherapy, devices, procedures, behavioral, and complementary approaches.
  • Outcome measures include pain intensity, functional status, and quality of life.
  • Participation in pain research contributes to developing safer pain management options.
