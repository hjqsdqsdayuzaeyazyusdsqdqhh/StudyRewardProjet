How to Find Paid Clinical Trials Near You

Proven strategies for locating clinical trials in your area that offer compensation for your time and participation.

By James Rodriguez May 28, 2026

Proven strategies for locating clinical trials in your area that offer compensation for your time and participation.

Using Online Clinical Trial Directories

Several online directories help you find clinical trials. ClinicalTrials.gov is the largest database, maintained by the National Institutes of Health. StudyReward also provides a searchable directory of active studies. These platforms allow you to filter by condition, location, and status. Most directories are free to use and updated regularly with new studies.

Searching by Location and Condition

To find studies near you, start by searching for your condition or as a healthy volunteer. Filter by your state or city to see locally available options. Many directories allow you to narrow results by study phase, status, and age group. Bookmark promising studies and check back regularly, as new trials are posted throughout the year.

Working with Your Healthcare Provider

Your healthcare provider can be a valuable resource for finding clinical trials. Doctors often know about research studies being conducted at their institution or in their network. Ask your provider if any trials might be appropriate for your situation. They can also help you evaluate whether participation is aligned with your treatment goals.

Key Takeaways

  • ClinicalTrials.gov and StudyReward are free tools for finding studies by condition and location.
  • Filter by state and city to find trials near you.
  • Your healthcare provider can help identify appropriate trials and evaluate participation.
  • New clinical trials are added regularly, so check back frequently.

Frequently Asked Questions
