Rare Disease Clinical Trials: Finding Treatment Options

How to find clinical trials for rare diseases and gain access to experimental treatments through research participation.

By Attorney Rachel Green April 18, 2026

How to find clinical trials for rare diseases and gain access to experimental treatments through research participation.

Challenges in Rare Disease Research

Rare diseases each affect a small percentage of the population, which creates unique challenges for clinical research. Finding enough participants for statistically meaningful trials can be difficult. However, regulatory incentives such as orphan drug designation encourage pharmaceutical companies to develop treatments for rare conditions.

Finding Rare Disease Trials

Patients with rare diseases can find clinical trials through specialized databases, patient advocacy organizations, rare disease foundations, and research networks. Many rare disease organizations maintain registries that connect patients with relevant research opportunities.

Key Takeaways

  • Rare disease trials face unique challenges due to small patient populations.
  • Orphan drug incentives encourage development of rare disease treatments.
  • Patient advocacy organizations are valuable resources for finding trials.
  • Participation in rare disease research can provide access to experimental treatments.
